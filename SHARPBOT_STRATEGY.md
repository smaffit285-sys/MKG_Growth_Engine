# Internal Autonomous Sharpening Venture Strategy

> Internal planning document. Do not use this language in customer-facing MKG, Sharp After Dark, membership, workshop, market, or commercial sales materials.

## Core Thesis

Miami Knife Guy is the trust, data, workflow, and human-performance laboratory. The autonomous kiosk venture is the later-scale automation endpoint.

The path is not to jump directly from local sharpening to robotics. The path is to use MKG to generate:
- sharpening video data
- labeled edge-condition data
- customer trust data
- B2B operational proof
- training-system insights
- quality-control benchmarks
- market objection data
- brand authority

Then convert those assets into a robotic sharpening kiosk venture.

---

## Intended End State

A fully autonomous AI-driven robotic knife-sharpening kiosk network.

Target deployment:
- Minimum 5,000 units nationwide
- Concentrated in and around metropolitan centers
- Located in grocery stores, Costco-style retailers, culinary retail, and other high-frequency customer environments

Customer workflow:
1. Customer brings knives while shopping
2. Customer drops knives into secure intake
3. Kiosk identifies, assesses, and sharpens knives autonomously
4. Customer receives automated completion message
5. Customer pays digitally
6. Customer retrieves knives from automated locker system

Product experience:
- Live video feed of sharpening
- UGC and customer proof display
- Digital status updates
- Secure chain-of-custody workflow
- Retail-friendly unattended service model

## Technical Direction

Target capabilities:
- Sub-millimeter precision
- AI-driven blade assessment
- Multi-sensor sharpening feedback loop
- 8K video capture
- Ultrasonic hardness testing
- Pressure sensing
- Accelerometers
- Additional sensors as required for angle, vibration, heat, debris, and quality control
- Automated edge-quality validation
- Robotic handling and sharpening motion control
- Locker/payment/SMS workflow integration

## MKG as Data Engine

Every sharpening performed by MKG should eventually become a training opportunity.

Data to capture:
- Before video
- During sharpening video
- After video
- Blade type
- Steel type when known
- Brand/model when known
- Edge condition
- Damage type
- Sharpening angle
- Abrasive progression
- Time per knife
- Sharpening pressure patterns
- Pass count
- Technician notes
- Customer satisfaction
- Rework events
- Review outcome

Use cases:
- Computer vision training
- Blade classification
- Damage detection
- Quality prediction
- Human training feedback
- Robotic motion planning
- Consumer trust content

## Human Training System

Before full automation, build a human sharpening training system that shortens the feedback loop.

Concept:
- Haptic feedback gloves
- Red light / green light guidance system
- Angle feedback
- Pressure feedback
- Motion consistency feedback
- Pass-count guidance
- Error correction prompts
- Technician scoring

Purpose:
- Train additional sharpeners faster
- Standardize MKG quality
- Build sensor-feedback datasets
- Discover which human variables matter most before robotizing them
- Support regional scale to 10 sharpeners

## Critical Risks

### Trust Risk
Customers must believe the machine will not damage valuable knives.

### Liability Risk
Knife damage, theft, injury, sanitation, and custody must be tightly controlled.

### Technical Risk
The system must handle real-world knife variability, not ideal test blades only.

### Unit Economics Risk
The kiosk must generate enough repeat volume per location to justify deployment and maintenance.

### Maintenance Risk
Robotic sharpening creates dust, wear, abrasive replacement, calibration, cleaning, and sensor-maintenance requirements.

### Retail Partner Risk
Large retailers will require proof of safety, reliability, revenue, low staff burden, and brand fit.
